Soil Types in Community Gardens

Understanding your soil is the first step to successful gardening

Soil provides plants with essential nutrients, physical support, and water. Different plants require different soil conditions to thrive. Understanding your soil type is crucial for successful gardening.

Sandy Soil

Characteristics: Gritty texture, drains quickly, low in nutrients

Best for: Drought-tolerant plants like carrots, potatoes, and rosemary

Improvement: Add organic matter to improve water retention

Clay Soil

Characteristics: Sticky when wet, hard when dry, rich in nutrients

Best for: Plants that need moisture like broccoli, cabbage, and asters

Improvement: Add sand and organic matter to improve drainage

Loamy Soil

Characteristics: Balanced texture, ideal mixture, retains moisture

Best for: Most vegetables and fruits including tomatoes, peppers, and strawberries

Improvement: Maintain with regular compost additions

Silty Soil

Characteristics: Soft and soapy, holds moisture, fertile

Best for: Moisture-loving plants like willows and dogwoods

Improvement: Add organic matter to prevent compaction

Testing Your Soil

Before planting, it's important to test your soil to understand its composition and pH level. Here's how:

DIY Jar Test

Mix soil with water in a jar, shake, and let settle to see layers of sand, silt, and clay.

pH Test Kit

Available at garden centers, these kits measure soil acidity/alkalinity.

Professional Test

Send samples to a lab for comprehensive nutrient analysis.
